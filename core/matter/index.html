Why These Partnerships Matter

These organizations define expectations, ensure accountability through audits and reports, and protect member well‑being. Their oversight directly supports KHS in providing quality, compliant healthcare services.

  • They set the rules: CMS, DHCS and DMHC outline what we must do, while NCQA outlines what we should do for best practices.
  • They assess our performance: Through audits, surveys, or reporting requirements, they validate KHS (and by extension our delegates) is following the rules.
  • They protect our members: All these bodies focus on member protections. By working with them, we further KHS’ mission to serve our community.
  • They impact our accreditation and contracts: Good standing with NCQA can:
    • Influence our potential accreditation status.
    • Compliance with DHCS and DMHC keeps our license and contract secure.
    • Compliance with CMS is mandatory for any Medicare line of business.
